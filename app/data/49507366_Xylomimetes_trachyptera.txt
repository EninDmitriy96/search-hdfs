Xylomimetes trachyptera is a moth in the family Xyloryctidae. It was described by Turner in 1900. It is found in Australia, where it has been recorded from Queensland. The wingspan is about . The forewings are brown whitish, suffused with reddish brown and fuscous brown, with five or six tufts of raised scales. There is a dark-fuscous oblique mark on the costa at one-fourth and paler fuscous marks on the costa at the middle, three-fourths, and two more between the last and the apex. There is also all ill-defined blackish streak in the disc above the middle, and several fine blackish streaks along the veins in the posterior portion of the disc.
